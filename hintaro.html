The Dice

Each player rolls two Chazwa dice. Faces show Tukar, Kulro, both combined, or blank. One shared Hintaro die shows Hin, Taro, or blank. See the DICE KEY tab for what every face looks like and means.

Round Flow

  • Everyone antes into the pot.
  • Roll your two Chazwa dice.
  • A betting round: call, raise, or fold.
  • Each remaining player may reroll exactly one die.
  • The Hintaron throws the Hintaro die: a Hin cancels one Tukar for everyone, a Taro cancels one Kulro.
  • Best ranked hand wins the pot. The Hintaron role passes left each round.

Hintaro Score Ranking

1Tukar to Kulro
2Quadro-Kulro
3Tukar-Tukar
4Kulro-Kulro

Any other combination cannot win. Ties split the pot; if nobody ranks, the pot carries to next round.

Your Chazwa Dice (2 rolled per player)

Blank — worth nothing (2 of the 6 faces)
Tukar — worth 1 Tukar
Kulro — worth 1 Kulro
Tukar + Kulro — one die worth both: 1 Tukar and 1 Kulro
Kulro-Kulro — one die worth 2 Kulro

The Shared Hintaro Die (thrown once, affects everyone)

Blank — no effect (2 of the 6 faces)
Hin — cancels 1 Tukar, table-wide
Taro — cancels 1 Kulro, table-wide

Cancelling

HIN ✕ TUKAR
TARO ✕ KULRO

Only one pip is cancelled, not a whole die. If a die shows Tukar + Kulro and a Hin cancels its Tukar, the Kulro on that same die still counts. During the Hintaro throw, watch for a red targeting flash on the exact die that loses a pip — a small red badge stays on it afterward showing which symbol was cancelled.
